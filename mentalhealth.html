FAQs
What is mental health?

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It involves the ability to handle stress, relate to others, and make decisions.

What are common mental health disorders?

Common mental health disorders include anxiety disorders, depression, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, and eating disorders.

What are the treatment options for mental health disorders?

Treatment may include therapy (such as cognitive-behavioral therapy or talk therapy), medication prescribed by a psychiatrist, lifestyle changes, and support groups.

What types of mental health professionals are there?

Mental health professionals include psychiatrists, psychologists, counselors, therapists, and social workers. Each has different training and specialties.
